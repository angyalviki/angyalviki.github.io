Reconsolidated Memories, 2023

Reconsolidated Memories is an interactive video installation, that was exhibited in 2023 at the Quantified Perspectives: Rethinking Data Narratives group exhibition in Esch-sur-Alzette, Luxembourg and it was part of the Resonating Selves-Interface Cultures student exhibition at Ars Electronica Festival 2023 in Linz.

Reconsolidation

Recalling a memory from our past involves various areas of the brain, as memories are distributed throughout our neural networks. While retrieving a memory, it becomes susceptible to modification. Upon subsequent retrievals, we recall the altered version from the previous retrieval rather than the initial experience itself, which is known as reconsolidation.

The installation uses pictures from the artistâ€™s personal archive from the age of 2,5 to illustrate how memories are processed and manipulated by the brain.

How it works

The images are constantly distorted and morphed together. This emphasizes the idea that our memories are not static, but sensitive to change and constantly evolving. The longer the visitors watch, the more distorted the memories become, reflecting the phenomenon of reconsolidation.
